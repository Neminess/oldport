My name is Noora Hiltunen and I am a student at Taitotalo.

How I ended up here

Like most people, I also had big, unrealistic dreams and expectations about my adult life. I always imagined myself travelling the world and being a famous horseback-rider, a vet adn a singer.

Obviously those were teenage-dreams and none of those happened. Over the years I have worked multiple jobs, learning about different career-options along the way.

My previous jobs and schools include pastry-school, warehouse-worker (with a half-degree in that area), a kitchen worker, an office-asisstant/cleaner and working as a clerk at a gamestore.

All of those taught me different things about myself and the working environments. When I ended up jobless and didn't get into any school I applied to, the social workers of Työkkäri introduced me to a place called Kuntoutussäätiö.

At Kuntoutussäätiö

During my time at Kuntoutussäätiö(which can be best described as a "rehabilitation center", I got introduced to HTML/CSS, video-editing and photography.

I learned how a basic HTML-page works and what CSS does. The staff there was extremely encouraging, friendly and all-around great people.

That is where the spark for coding was planted. Eventually my time at Kuntoutussäätiö came to an end and I applied to Tietix.

Tietix

Tietix is a workshop focused on computers, although they do have a packing-faction as well where they package incoming merch that is then sent to Kierrätyskeskus etc places for sale.

I went to the coding-side/computer-side of Tietix. There, I was taught the basics of Python, got a small list of resources to use for studying and practice-projects.

Tietix is the main reason for my current situation. I never thought I could be a coder, but the encouragement given there, and at Kuntoutussäätiö, gave me the kick to start studying more.

When I had to leave Tietix, I applied to two different schools: Omnia and Taitotalo. I wasn't accepted to Omnia, but got a place at Taitotalo (which, funny enough, is an organisation that came to be from the unification of AmiEdu and AEL. Both are my former schools).

All in all, it's been a long road to get here and I can wholeheartedly say, that without those two places that helped me find my own way, I wouldn't be where I am now.
